Title: The solar system

Our solar system includes the Sun, planets, moons, comets, asteroids, ice, and dwarf planets. The planets orbit the Sun in elliptical paths. 
Planets
1. Mercury: The planet closest to the Sun, with an eccentric orbit that helped confirm Einstein's theory of relativity 
2. Venus: A planet that has evolved from habitable conditions to its current state 
3. Earth: The third planet from the Sun, with a central core, rocky mantle, and solid crust 
4. Mars: The fourth planet from the Sun, about half the size of Earth 
5. Jupiter: The largest planet in our solar system, a gaseous giant that's more than twice as large as all the other planets combined 
6. Saturn: One of the largest planets, mostly made of hydrogen and helium 
7. Uranus: The seventh planet from the Sun, a cyan-colored world tipped on its side 
8. Neptune: The farthest planet from the Sun, with the most meteorologically active atmosphere in the solar system 

Dwarf planets 
    1. Pluto: A dwarf planet located in the Trans-Neptunian region, beyond Neptune
